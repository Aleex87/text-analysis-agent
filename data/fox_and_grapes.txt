A hungry fox was wandering through a vineyard one hot afternoon. As he walked, he noticed a large bunch of grapes hanging from a high vine. The grapes looked ripe, juicy, and delicious.

The fox stepped back, took a run, and jumped as high as he could. But he missed the grapes. He tried again, jumping even higher this time, but still he could not reach them.

Again and again, the fox tried to get the grapes. He jumped, stretched, and leaped, but each time he failed. After many attempts, he grew tired and frustrated.

Finally, the fox gave up. As he walked away, he muttered to himself, “Those grapes are probably sour anyway.”

And so, he left, pretending that he did not want the grapes at all.

The moral of the story is that it is easy to despise what you cannot have.
